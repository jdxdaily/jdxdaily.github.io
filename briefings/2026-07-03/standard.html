TL;DR

Yesterday's US session saw the Dow Jones index rise +1.14% to 52,900.07, while the S&P 500 held steady at 7,483.24. The Nasdaq 100, however, fell -1.61% to 29,329.21. Beneath the surface, sector rotation favored financials and consumer staples, with XLF and XLP rising +1.53% and +2.03%, respectively.

A closer look at the large-cap movers reveals AAPL surging +4.84% and TSLA plummeting -7.49%. The 10-year yield jumped +2.58% to 4.48, while gold rose +1.87% to 4,189.60. These moves suggest a nuanced market environment, with investors seeking both growth and safety.
