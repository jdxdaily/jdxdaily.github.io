US Stocks End Mixed as Tech Selloff Counters Healthcare Gains

A wider read of yesterday's session — sector rotation, pre-market action, watchlist scan, and what to watch into the US open at 21:30 HKT.

10-minute read · Topics: Macro · Geopolitics · Earnings · Sectors · Watchlist Download PDF

TL;DR

The US market closed mixed, with the S&P 500 down -0.05% and the Nasdaq 100 falling -1.09%. Below the surface, sector rotation favored healthcare and consumer staples, while tech stocks weighed on the market. The VIX fell -2.54% to 18.41, indicating a decrease in market volatility.

A closer look at the session reveals that the Russell 2000 outperformed, rising +0.07% as investors sought out smaller-cap stocks. The Dow Jones was down -0.09%, with JPM and GS contributing to the decline. In contrast, AAPL and MSFT were among the top gainers, up +3.14% and +5.71%, respectively.
