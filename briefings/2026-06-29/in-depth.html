TL;DR & Editorial Take

The US market ended the latest session with mixed results, as the S&P 500 closed at 7,354.02, down -0.05%, while the Nasdaq 100 fell -1.09% to 29,118.24. Key takeaways include:

  • The Russell 2000 outperformed, closing +0.07% at 3,010.08, indicating small-cap resilience.
  • The VIX decreased -2.54% to 18.41, suggesting a decrease in market volatility.

These movements reflect ongoing market dynamics, with investors weighing economic indicators, sector performance, and global events.

The editorial take is that while the market shows signs of resilience, particularly in small-cap stocks and certain sectors, the overall trend remains cautious due to ongoing economic uncertainties and geopolitical tensions.
