TL;DR & Editorial Take

The US market saw a mixed session, with the Nasdaq 100 rising +0.33% to 29,825.11 and the Dow Jones gaining +0.29% to 52,637.01. The Russell 2000 fell -0.49% to 2,977.81. Key takeaways include:

  • The 10Y Yield jumped +0.66% to 4.57, its highest level in weeks.
  • WTI Crude dropped -0.93% to 71.41, while Gold fell -0.41% to 4,113.70.
  • The DXY rose +0.03% to 100.97, while BTC slipped -0.07% to 64,081.82.

The market's mixed performance suggests that investors are weighing the impact of rising yields and commodity prices on the economy. The Nasdaq 100's gain was driven by strong performances from NVDA and META, which rose +4.03% and +5.97%, respectively.

Looking ahead, investors will be watching the 10Y Yield and WTI Crude prices closely, as well as the upcoming earnings reports from major companies. The market's technical levels will also be in focus, with the SPY and QQQ approaching key resistance levels.
