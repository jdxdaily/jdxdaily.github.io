TL;DR & Editorial Take

The US market experienced a significant downturn, with the S&P 500 closing at 7,408.30, down -1.21%. This decline was mirrored in other major indices, including the Nasdaq 100 and Dow Jones. The VIX also saw a substantial increase, rising +12.38% to 18.70.

The primary drivers of this market movement appear to be a combination of factors, including rising interest rates and a decrease in risk appetite. The 10Y yield increased by +0.99% to 4.70, while WTI crude oil prices dipped -0.27% to 91.94.

Key points to consider:

  • The current market volatility is expected to continue, driven by economic uncertainty and geopolitical tensions.
  • Sector performance was mixed, with XLI and XLV experiencing gains, while XLY and XLC suffered significant losses.
  • Large-cap stocks, such as AAPL and MSFT, saw declines, with TSLA experiencing a notable -14.52% drop.
