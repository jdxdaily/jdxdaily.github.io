TL;DR & Editorial Take

The US market took a hit in the last session, with the SPY down -0.45% and the QQQ down -1.77%. The main culprits were rising rates and oil prices, which spiked +2.57% to 72.25. Our take: this could be the start of a larger correction, but for now, it's just a dip.

Key points to watch:

  • Rates: The 10Y yield is now at 4.53, up +0.98% from the previous day.
  • Oil: WTI crude is up +2.57% to 72.25, a significant move that could impact inflation and growth.
  • Tech stocks: The XLK sector ETF is down -2.39%, with NVDA and MSFT among the biggest losers.

Our editorial take is that this dip could be a buying opportunity, but investors should be cautious and watch for further signs of weakness.
