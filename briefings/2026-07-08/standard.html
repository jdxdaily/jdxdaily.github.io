US Stocks Slip as Rates and Oil Prices Rise

A wider read of yesterday's session — sector rotation, pre-market action, watchlist scan, and what to watch into the US open at 21:30 HKT.

10-minute read · Topics: Macro · Geopolitics · Earnings · Sectors · Watchlist Download PDF

TL;DR

The S&P 500 fell -0.45% to 7,503.85 as rising rates and oil prices weighed on investor sentiment. Beneath the surface, the NVDA and META outperformed, while TSLA was the largest drag on the index.

A closer look at sector rotation reveals that energy and healthcare were the only sectors to finish in the green, with XLE and XLV rising +2.84% and +1.53%, respectively. The VIX jumped +3.60% to 16.13, indicating increased market volatility.
