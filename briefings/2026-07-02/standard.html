Nasdaq 100 Leads Decline as Tech Stocks Weigh on US Markets

A wider read of yesterday's session — sector rotation, pre-market action, watchlist scan, and what to watch into the US open at 21:30 HKT.

10-minute read · Topics: Macro · Geopolitics · Earnings · Sectors · Watchlist Download PDF

TL;DR

The US market saw a decline in the previous session, led by the Nasdaq 100 which fell by 1.54%. The S&P 500 and Dow Jones also declined, albeit at a slower pace. Below the surface, the rotation out of tech stocks and into financials and consumer staples was notable, with XLF and XLY posting gains of 2.18% and 0.69% respectively.

The move was also accompanied by a rise in the 10Y Yield, which increased by 2.36% to 4.47. This suggests that investors are becoming increasingly cautious about the outlook for the economy, and are seeking safer assets. The VIX also rose, gaining 0.85% to 16.59, indicating increased volatility in the market.
