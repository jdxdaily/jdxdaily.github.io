US Stocks Slide as Oil Surges and VIX Spikes

A wider read of yesterday's session — sector rotation, pre-market action, watchlist scan, and what to watch into the US open at 21:30 HKT.

10-minute read · Topics: Macro · Geopolitics · Earnings · Sectors · Watchlist Download PDF

TL;DR

The S&P 500 fell 0.79% to 7,515.34 as the VIX spiked 14.17% to 17.16, indicating increased market volatility. The surge in oil prices, with WTI crude rising 11.41% to 79.56, contributed to the decline in stocks. Below the surface, the NVDA and TSLA declines weighed on the Nasdaq 100, which dropped 1.88% to 29,264.10.

The 10Y yield rose 0.88% to 4.61, while the DXY index increased 0.20% to 101.17. The gold price fell 2.01% to 4,021.60. The mixed market signals suggest investors are cautious ahead of the US open.
