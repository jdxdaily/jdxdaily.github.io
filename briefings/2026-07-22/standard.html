US Stocks Rise as Tech Leads the Charge, Nasdaq 100 Up +1.93%

A wider read of yesterday's session — sector rotation, pre-market action, watchlist scan, and what to watch into the US open at 21:30 HKT.

TL;DR

The US market saw a broad-based rally yesterday, with the Nasdaq 100 leading the charge, up +1.93%. The S&P 500 and Dow Jones also closed higher, up +0.89% and +0.74%, respectively. Beneath the surface, the rally was driven by a combination of factors, including a decline in volatility, with the VIX down -8.58%, and a surge in tech stocks, led by NVDA, up +1.97%.

Looking ahead, the market will be closely watching the upcoming earnings season, with several major companies set to report this week. The economic calendar is also packed, with key data releases on inflation, retail sales, and housing starts. Meanwhile, the geopolitical landscape remains uncertain, with ongoing tensions between major world powers. As such, investors will need to stay vigilant and adapt to changing market conditions.
