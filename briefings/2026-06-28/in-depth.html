TL;DR & Editorial Take

The US markets closed the week on a cautious note, with the S&P 500 down -0.05% and the Nasdaq 100 down -1.09%. The main drivers of this move were the continued rise in rates, with the 10Y Yield at 4.37, and the decline in commodities, with WTI Crude down -3.74% to 69.23. Key points to consider include:

  • The Russell 2000 was a notable exception, ending the week up +0.07% at 3,010.08.
  • Sector performance was mixed, with XLV and XLY outperforming, up +3.03% and +0.90% respectively.
  • The VIX closed down -2.54% at 18.41, indicating a decrease in market volatility.

The week ahead will be closely watched for any signs of a shift in market sentiment, particularly with regards to the ongoing rate hike cycle and its impact on the broader economy.

In terms of key events, the upcoming week will see a number of important data releases, including GDP and inflation figures, which will be closely watched for any signs of a slowdown in economic growth.
