TL;DR & Editorial Take

The US market experienced a significant downturn in the last session, with the S&P 500 declining -1.01% and the Nasdaq 100 falling -1.49%. The VIX surged 12.19%, indicating increased market volatility. Key takeaways include:

  • The energy sector was a notable exception, with the XLE ETF rising 1.16% due to the 3.58% increase in WTI crude oil prices.
  • The 10Y yield decreased -0.61%, potentially signaling a shift in investor sentiment towards safer assets.
  • The DXY index remained relatively stable, increasing 0.02%, as the dollar's value against other currencies was largely unchanged.

The current market conditions suggest a high level of uncertainty, with investors seeking safer assets and the potential for further volatility. The surge in oil prices and the decline in the 10Y yield are key factors to watch in the coming sessions.

The editorial take is that the market is entering a period of heightened volatility, and investors should be prepared for potential fluctuations in the days ahead. The increase in oil prices and the decline in the 10Y yield are likely to have a significant impact on the market, and investors should closely monitor these factors.
