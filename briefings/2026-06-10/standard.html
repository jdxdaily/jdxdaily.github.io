US Stocks End Mixed as Tech Selloff Weighs on Nasdaq

A wider read of yesterday's session — sector rotation, pre-market action, watchlist scan, and what to watch into the US open at 21:30 HKT.

TL;DR

The US stock market ended the session mixed, with the Dow Jones gaining 0.17% and the S&P 500 losing 0.26%, while the Nasdaq 100 fell 1.12% due to a tech selloff. Below the surface, the Russell 2000 outperformed with a 0.41% gain, and the VIX jumped 5.02% to 19.87. The 10Y yield declined 0.53% to 4.53, and WTI crude oil dropped 1.88% to 89.58.

Notable movers included AAPL down 3.64%, MSFT down 2.02%, and TSLA down 3.00%. The sector ETFs showed a mixed picture, with XLF up 0.94% and XLK down 1.85%. The market is waiting for the next catalyst, with earnings season approaching and the Fed's next move uncertain.
