TL;DR & Editorial Take

The US stock market rebounded yesterday, led by tech giants such as AAPL and MSFT, which rose +4.01% and +2.78% respectively. The S&P 500 closed at 7,572.40, up +0.38% for the day. The market's rebound was driven by a combination of factors, including a decline in the 10Y Yield to 4.55 and a rise in WTI Crude to 79.60.

The editorial take is that the market's rebound is a positive sign, but it's too early to tell if it's a sustainable trend. The market is still facing headwinds, including a slowing economy and rising interest rates. Investors should remain cautious and keep a close eye on the market's technical levels and sector internals.

Key points to watch include:

  • The S&P 500's ability to hold above its 50-day moving average
  • The Nasdaq 100's performance, which is closely tied to the tech sector
  • The 10Y Yield's impact on the market, as a decline in yields could lead to a rise in stock prices
