TL;DR & Editorial Take

The US market saw a broad-based rally yesterday, with the SPY and QQQ both closing higher. Key drivers included a decline in yields and a rebound in oil prices. We highlight three key points to watch:

  • Yield curve dynamics: The 10Y yield dropped -0.52% to 4.59, while the 2Y yield remained relatively stable.
  • Sector rotation: The XLK and XLE sector ETFs outperformed, gaining +1.29% and +0.37% respectively.
  • Crypto momentum: BTC and ETH prices surged +4.06% and +5.80% respectively, driven by renewed risk appetite.

Our editorial take is that the current market environment is characterized by a delicate balance between growth concerns and inflation expectations. Investors should remain vigilant and monitor key indicators for signs of a potential shift in market sentiment.

In conclusion, the US market's risk-on sentiment is driven by a combination of factors, including declining yields, rebounding oil prices, and surging crypto momentum. As we look ahead to the week, investors should focus on key events, including the upcoming Fed meeting and earnings reports from major companies.
