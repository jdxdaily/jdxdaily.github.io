US Markets Tumble as Risk-Off Sentiment Takes Hold

A wider read of yesterday's session — sector rotation, pre-market action, watchlist scan, and what to watch into the US open at 21:30 HKT.

10-minute read · Topics: Macro · Geopolitics · Earnings · Sectors · Watchlist Download PDF

TL;DR

The US market sell-off deepened, with the S&P 500 plummeting 2.64% to 7,383.74 and the Nasdaq 100 tumbling 4.77% to 28,957.60. Beneath the surface, sector rotation revealed a flight to safety, with XLP and XLV outperforming. The VIX surged 39.68% to 21.51, indicating heightened volatility.

A closer examination of the market's internals reveals that the sell-off was broad-based, with 9 out of 11 sector ETFs closing in the red. The 10Y Yield jumped 1.32% to 4.54, while WTI Crude and Gold prices rose 2.21% and 0.65%, respectively.
