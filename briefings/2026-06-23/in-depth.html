TL;DR & Editorial Take

The US market session ended on a mixed note, with the Dow Jones bucking the trend to post a +0.29% gain. The S&P 500 and Nasdaq 100 closed in the red, down -0.37% and -0.19% respectively. Key takeaways from the session include:

  • The Russell 2000 outperformed, closing +0.83% higher.
  • The VIX spiked +5.37% to 17.28, indicating increased market volatility.
  • WTI crude oil prices plummeted -3.36% to 74.03, weighing on energy stocks.

The mixed market performance reflects ongoing uncertainty and volatility in the global economy. Investors remain cautious, with the VIX spike indicating increased risk appetite. The Russell 2000's outperformance suggests a potential shift towards smaller-cap stocks.

Looking ahead, investors will be closely watching the upcoming Fed meeting and economic data releases for clues on the direction of interest rates and the overall health of the economy. The current market environment is characterized by heightened uncertainty, and investors should remain vigilant and adaptable.
