US Markets Tumble as Tech Stocks Lead Decline

A 5-minute read of yesterday's US session and today's catalysts — what to watch before pre-market opens at 21:30 HKT.

2-3 page read · Topics: Macro · Geopolitics · Earnings Download PDF

TL;DR

The US markets experienced a significant decline yesterday, with the NVDA and TSLA leading the drop. The S&P 500 closed at 7,365.46, down -1.44%, while the Nasdaq 100 plummeted -3.29% to 29,347.27.

Today, investors will be watching the 10Y Yield, which surged +0.94% to 4.49, and the VIX, which jumped +12.79% to 19.49. The DXY also rose +0.38% to 101.40.

Top large-cap movers

TSLA led the decline, plummeting -5.79% to 381.61, followed by NVDA, which dropped -4.13% to 200.04. On the other hand, MSFT rose +1.80% to 373.94.

Spotlight

Company Earnings Revenue
NVDA 1.23 7.32B

NVDA reported earnings of 1.23 per share, beating expectations, but revenue fell short at 7.32B. The stock price dropped -4.13% to 200.04 despite the positive earnings surprise.

The company's guidance for the next quarter was also cautious, citing ongoing supply chain issues and softening demand. This led to a decline in investor sentiment, contributing to the stock's decline.
