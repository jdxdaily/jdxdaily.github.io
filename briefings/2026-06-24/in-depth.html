TL;DR & Editorial Take

The US markets experienced a significant decline in the previous session, with the S&P 500 and Nasdaq 100 dropping -1.44% and -3.29% respectively. The sell-off was broad-based, with all major indices and sectors participating in the decline. Our editorial take is that the market is experiencing a risk-off sentiment, driven by concerns over the global economic outlook and the impact of rising interest rates.

The key factors driving the market's decline include the increase in the 10Y Yield to 4.49, the decline in WTI Crude to 72.77, and the rise in the DXY to 101.40. These factors have contributed to a decline in investor sentiment, leading to a sell-off in risk assets.

The main points to focus on are:

  • The S&P 500 and Nasdaq 100 are trading below their 50-day moving averages, indicating a bearish trend.
  • The VIX has surged to 19.49, indicating increased market volatility.
  • The 10Y Yield is trading at 4.49, its highest level in recent months.
